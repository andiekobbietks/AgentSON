The problem

Your AI sessions are trapped.

🔒

Vendor lock-in

Every AI coding agent stores session data in its own proprietary SQLite database. 21 tables here, 20 there. No standard. No portability. Your data belongs to whoever built the tool.

Format churn

Formats change without warning

Chrome removed the export button in version 149. Sessions are still in your profile — but locked in a JSON blob no other tool can read. Your AI history now belongs to a vendor who can change the format at any commit.

Can't train

No path from transcript to adapter

You have hundreds of useful sessions. But there's no way to turn them into training data. No standard export. No fine-tuning pipeline. Your best work dies with the tool.

Hardware trap

Vendor marketing picks your machine

They sell you "AI capability" based on what they can ship, not what you need. You end up paying for cloud GPUs when a £95 Raspberry Pi could run the same adapter.

One format, three layers

Same data, any hardware.

Same corpus, any hardware. One format that travels from your laptop to a Pi to an RTX 4090 — no conversion, no cloud, no account.

1

Format

Portable transcript — typed JSON schema capturing user-query, thought, action, answer, side-effect, context. Works across Cursor, Claude, DevTools, AI Studio. Vendor-neutral. MIT.

v0.1
2

Train

Edge fine-tuning — same corpus fine-tunes on Unsloth (GPU), Olive + ONNX (NPU/CPU), or Google Colab (no GPU). Adapters are 50–200 MB. Sync between devices. Federate without sharing data.

3

Deploy

Run anywhere — adapter + ONNX INT4 → runs on RTX 4090, M3 Max, Snapdragon X Elite, Raspberry Pi 5, or your browser. Live community-submitted numbers for each. No subscription. No cloud.
